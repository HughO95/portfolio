Imperial Hotel

The four-star Imperial Hotel Copenhagen is located in the heart of Copenhagen, only two minutes' walk from the Tivoli Gardens, the City Hall Square and Strøget, the famous pedestrian street. The Imperial Hotel has an intense atmosphere that is also recognised by residents of Copenhagen, who often enjoys the restaurant, the brasserie and the bar.


Wakeup Hotel

Scroll back to the top


Wakeup Hotel is part of the Arp-Hansen Hotel Group, Wakeup Copenhagen consists of two new and modern budget hotels in Copenhagen.
Whether you are here on business or travelling with friends and family, Wakeup Copenhagen is the ideal choice for everyone looking for an affordable stay in the city centre.
Wakeup Copenhagen in Borgergade is as central as it gets. Located just a few blocks down from King’s Garden, you’ll have Nørreport Station, the big shopping streets, Nyhavn and many other points of interest just a few minutes away.

Bedwood Hostel

Scroll back to the top


At Bedwood Hostel, you find that cozy hostel vibe in Copenhagen’s city center
In a quiet courtyard off the cobbled street of Nyhavn, you find Bedwood: one of the coziest accommodations for backpackers and other price-conscious travelers.
In the summer of 2015, the beautiful warehouse from 1756 got a complete overhaul, including new hand-built beds. The rooms and dorms accommodate from two to 12 guests, and there are curtains in all the beds securing more privacy than normally seen in dorms.

Københavns Cykelbørs

Scroll back to the top


Cykelbørsen was founded in 1881 and is the largest bike rental company in Denmark.
Here you can rent Raleigh 3-speed bikes, Nishiki touring bikes, children's bikes, bicycles with children's seats and tandems.
They also supply helmets, bike-bags and other accessories. Always bicycles on stock. Always high in quality and low in price.
Cykelbørsen is also where you find the world's only GPS-guided bike tours that let you discover Copenhagen at your own pace.
Bike the City tours are in English, French, German and Danish.
